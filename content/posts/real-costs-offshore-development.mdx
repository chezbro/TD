When companies consider offshore development, the immediate focus is often on the lower hourly rates. However, the true ROI extends far beyond simple labor costs.

## Direct Cost Savings

### Salary Comparisons

- US-based Senior Developer: $150,000-$200,000/year
- Equivalent Offshore Developer: $40,000-$60,000/year
- Savings: 60-75% on direct salary costs

### Additional Cost Reductions

- No healthcare benefits management
- Reduced office space requirements
- Lower equipment and software licensing costs
- Eliminated recruitment agency fees

## Hidden Benefits

### Scaling Flexibility

- Rapid team expansion capability
- No long-term commitments
- Ability to scale down without layoffs
- Access to specialized skills on-demand

### Time Zone Advantages

- 24/7 development cycle
- Faster project completion
- Round-the-clock customer support
- Extended working hours coverage

## Long-term Value

### Knowledge Base Expansion

- Diverse technical perspectives
- Global market insights
- Cross-cultural collaboration skills
- Extended technology stack expertise

### Competitive Advantage

- Faster time-to-market
- More features delivered
- Better customer support coverage
- Increased innovation capacity

## Investment Considerations

### Initial Setup Costs

- Team selection and hiring: $5,000-$10,000
- Infrastructure setup: $2,000-$5,000
- Training and onboarding: $3,000-$7,000

### Ongoing Management

- Communication tools: $50-100/person/month
- Project management software: $20-50/person/month
- Quality assurance processes: $1,000-2,000/month

## ROI Calculator Example

For a team of 5 developers:

### US-Based Costs (Annual)

- Salaries: $875,000
- Benefits: $175,000
- Office Space: $60,000
- Total: $1,110,000

### Offshore Costs (Annual)

- Salaries: $250,000
- Management Overhead: $50,000
- Tools & Infrastructure: $15,000
- Total: $315,000

### Annual Savings: $795,000
